History


The first trace of piercing dates back to the 9th century before Christ, and was found in Nimrud, Iraq. A stone relief depiction of man with a pierced ear of 7 to 11 diameter was found.
In ancient Egypt navel piercing were adorned by nobles royals to assert their high standing. Otherwise, naval piercing are a more recent fashion.
Ear and septum piercing were observed accross cultures. Warrior cultures in New Guinea and Solomon Isles were born by seasoned hunters.
Nose piercings were in high fashion In 15th century India.



Modern use


There has been a stigma on people bearing piercings they were seen as rebels or thugs. More recently piercing have become more acceptable socially and are seen as a way to express individuality.
